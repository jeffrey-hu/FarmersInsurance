# Key Facts — Pro-Family (Legal Context Excluded)

A running list of the facts of the case that favor the Lu/Hu family and cut against Farmers Insurance. Legal framing (bad faith, breach of contract, unfair claims practices, matching, etc.) is intentionally omitted — facts will be tied to legal arguments separately.

Organized to match the phases of `ClaimTimeline.md`.

## Phase 1 — Background & Prior Claims (2014–2023)

1. **Continuous, long-standing policyholder relationship.** The Lu/Hu family has been insured under Farmers policy 98055-49-65 (Fire Insurance Exchange) without interruption since 10/22/2014 — roughly ten years at the time of the 7/13/2024 loss. *(Sources: `HomePolicy/2014_Application-PolicyDocument-980554965.pdf`; `HomePolicy/2023_Renewal.pdf`)*

2. **The 2020 hail claim's Xactimate scope (Farmers' own document) approved only 2 wood casement windows for the entire property.** No other windows, sashes, wraps, or glass were ever approved or paid under the 2020 claim. Any dents on any other window therefore cannot be attributed to the 2020 event under Farmers' own prior assessment. *(Source: `EventDocuments/FinalDocument_2020.pdf`, line items 1–4)*

3. **Gutters and downspouts were replaced after 9/13/2021.** Any hail damage visible on those replacement gutters necessarily occurred *after* 2021 — objective physical proof of post-2020 storm activity at the property. *(Source: `EventDocuments/2025-01-31-ResponseToFarmer-01-31-25-StormsAfter2020.pdf`; underlying contractor text messages dated 9/13/2021 not yet in project folder)*

4. **2023 renewal confirmed the policy in force on the loss date** with Coverage A of $886,000 and an Owner Occupied / Primary Residence classification. The property was the family's primary home, actively insured, with a "New Roof" discount applied (confirming prior repair compliance). *(Source: `HomePolicy/2023_Renewal.pdf`)*

## Phase 2 — The Storm & Claim Filing (July–August 2024)

1. **An official NWS storm event is documented on the loss date at the loss location.** NWS Twin Cities/Chanhassen confirms multiple rounds of severe thunderstorms across Minnesota on 7/13–7/14/2024, with hail up to 3.5" in east-central Minnesota and up to 4" near Monticello/Big Lake. The initial observation of hail damage came from an independent contractor — not from a homeowner looking for a payout. *(Source: `EventDocuments/Copy of ComunicationLogs.docx.md`; `EventDocuments/11-19-complaint-to-dept-of-commerce.pdf` timeline)*

3. **The family opened the claim promptly once damage was discovered** and immediately cooperated with Farmers' chosen inspection process: calling for status on 8/19/2024 and proactively calling Hancock on 8/20/2024 to hand off Legacy's contact information so the inspection could be scheduled. *(Source: `EventDocuments/Copy of ComunicationLogs.docx.md`; `EventDocuments/2025-01-31-ResponseToFarmer.pdf` timeline)*

4. **Farmers chose Hancock Claims Consultants and Jayden Grimm for the first inspection on 8/29/2024.** The family did not select the inspector; Hancock was entirely Farmers' vendor of choice. *(Source: `EventDocuments/FarmersDenyLetter_09_03_2024.pdf` pp. 1–2; `EventDocuments/Copy of ComunicationLogs.docx.md`)*

## Phase 3 — First Denial, Reopening & Second Inspection (September 2024)

1. **The first denial was issued before any engineering analysis.** On 9/3/2024 Farmers denied the claim both by phone and by formal written letter (signed by Catastrophe Claims Rep Christian Dudley), based entirely on Hancock's 8/29 inspection. Prieve Engineering had not yet been engaged. Farmers reached and communicated a denial without developing any technical or engineering basis. *(Source: `EventDocuments/FarmersDenyLetter_09_03_2024.pdf` pp. 1–2)*

2. **The 9/3 written denial affirmatively stated Farmers found "no other damages" on the home.** The letter says verbatim that "interior water damage around your windows was observed. There were no other damages observed. There was no opening created by the direct force of wind or hail that could have occurred and allowed water to enter and cause immediate damage." This is critical: Farmers' first formal finding is that the home has no exterior damage anywhere. That finding is later contradicted by (a) Farmers' own adjuster on 9/6/2024, (b) the Prieve report (which shifts to an entirely different theory — pre-existing 2020 damage and wear/tear), and (c) the September 2025 appraisal award confirming hail damage to windows. The 9/3 denial therefore fixes Farmers to a baseline scope finding ("no other damages") that every subsequent Farmers position walks away from — the dispute is no longer *whether* hail damage exists, but *how much*, and Farmers has moved the line repeatedly. *(Source: `EventDocuments/FarmersDenyLetter_09_03_2024.pdf` pp. 1–2)*

3. **The family was proactively cooperative throughout the second inspection process** — submitting additional photos on 9/8 and 9/10, meeting with agent Peter Pietila on 9/11 for guidance on how to document damage, and accepting a third inspection by Farmers' chosen engineer. *(Source: `EventDocuments/Copy of ComunicationLogs.docx.md`; `EventDocuments/11-19-complaint-to-dept-of-commerce.pdf` timeline)*

4. **The 9/6/2024 Nic Brau inspection generated no produced written record.** On 9/6/2024 Nic Brau (Farmers' Claims Spec Rep Prop) inspected the property personally, found dents on windows alongside the family, and measured window dimensions. Farmers later states "the file does not contain a report on the requested date" (10/10/2025), and Nic Brau personally refused to release any notes from the inspection. An inspection in which Farmers' own adjuster personally observed dents and took measurements has no traceable written output in Farmers' file. *(Sources: `EventDocuments/Copy of ComunicationLogs.docx.md`; `EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/2025-09-18-PostAppraisalEmailChain.md`; `CaseContext/key-facts.md` §1)*

## Phase 4 — Prieve Inspection & Second Denial (October–November 2024)

1. **The 11/4/2024 denial rests on a completely different factual theory than the 9/3/2024 denial.** The 9/3 letter denied because "there were no other damages observed" and "no opening" — an interior-water-exclusion theory. The 11/4 letter denies because Prieve attributes observed damage to "wear/tear" and to windows "already included and paid" under the 2020 claim. The two denials' factual premises are incompatible: one says nothing is damaged, the other acknowledges damage exists but blames aging and prior storms. *(Sources: `EventDocuments/FarmersDenyLetter_09_03_2024.pdf` pp. 1–2; `EventDocuments/FarmersDenyLetter-11-4.pdf` p. 1)*

2. **Prieve's own report documents dents, marks, and indentations across most of the home.** Despite his "not consistent with hail" conclusion, Prieve's narrative describes dents and marks at: the front/west triple window above the main entry, the upper-west 3-unit bedroom window (photo A-45), the upper southwest bedroom window (A-64), the east family room bay projection (A-107, A-113, A-121), the upper-east window above the four-season porch (A-163), the four-season porch southeast/east/northeast/north windows (A-165, A-166, A-174), the kitchen east window, the main-level NE 3-unit window (A-199, A-201), the upper-north window above the kitchen, the rear/east garage window, and a siding penetration below the upper NE bedroom (photos A-26, A-27, A-212). The factual existence of damage across the home is effectively admitted — only the causation characterization is contested. *(Source: `EventDocuments/FarmersDenyLetter-11-4.pdf` pp. 4–9 (Prieve report pp. 3–6))*

3. **Prieve's report confirms the 2020 claim scope was exactly 2 windows** — "main level window to the left of the main entry and north facing window at the rear four season porch." This matches Farmers' own 2020 Xactimate. Prieve's own report therefore forecloses the "2020 already paid for these" defense as to every other dented window on the property. *(Sources: `EventDocuments/FarmersDenyLetter-11-4.pdf` p. 10 (Prieve conclusion 1); cross-reference `EventDocuments/FinalDocument_2020.pdf` line items 1–4)*

4. **Prieve affirmatively claimed there has been no hail in Eden Prairie after 2020.** His Weather Records section summarizes stormersite and the NOAA Storm Events Database as showing "most recent hail in Eden Prairie reported in 2020," and his conclusions rest on the premise that no recent hail has struck the property. This is a direct factual assertion — not a cautious hedge — and it is independently disproven by (a) the NWS Twin Cities/Chanhassen official storm report documenting severe thunderstorms across Minnesota on 7/13–7/14/2024 with a 48 MPH wind gust specifically recorded at Eden Prairie AP ASOS at 1:00 AM on 7/14; (b) stormersite data cited in the family's 1/31/2025 response showing hail reports near Eden Prairie in 2022, 2023, and 2024; (c) City of Eden Prairie building-permit records and the family's neighbor canvass (14 of 19 homeowners) confirming hail-driven exterior repairs in the neighborhood in 2023 and 2024; and (d) Farmers' own September 2025 appraisal award confirming hail as the cause of loss on the same property. Prieve's "no hail after 2020" claim is a factual error, and it adds another inconsistency to Farmers' shifting record of positions. *(Sources: `EventDocuments/FarmersDenyLetter-11-4.pdf` p. 9 (Weather Records) and p. 10 (Conclusion 1); `Evidence/Significant Wind and Hail in Minnesota July 13-14.pdf`; `EventDocuments/2025-01-31-ResponseToFarmer-01-31-25-StormsAfter2020.pdf`; `EventDocuments/2025-02-10-SecondResponseToFinalReport.pdf`; `EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/Appraisal_Result_Front.png`)*

5. **Prieve's dismissal of the east-side siding penetration depends on the assumption hail only arrives from the west.** Prieve states "recent storm activity in the area generally consists of storms moving from west to east with hail originating from a westerly direction. The penetration in siding appears at the east side, opposite typical origination of hail." This is a generalization, not an analysis of the 7/13–7/14/2024 event, which the NWS documents as multi-round thunderstorms including an overnight line with embedded large hail — conditions that produce multi-directional impacts. *(Sources: `EventDocuments/FarmersDenyLetter-11-4.pdf` p. 10 (Conclusion 4); `Evidence/Significant Wind and Hail in Minnesota July 13-14.pdf`)*

6. **Prieve is a hired, paid expert of Farmers.** The report states expressly: "Per the request of Nic Brau of Farmers Insurance, our office visited the subject property on October 9, 2024" and "This report is to be utilized only by the contracted party." *(Source: `EventDocuments/FarmersDenyLetter-11-4.pdf` pp. 4–5 (Prieve report Background section))*

7. **After the 11/4 denial, Farmers' adjuster refused to meet to discuss the engineer report.** On 11/8/2024 the family emailed Nic Brau requesting a meeting; on 11/11/2024 Nic refused by email stating "I have already responded with our finding/outcome in the letter/engineer report." *(Sources: `EventDocuments/Copy of ComunicationLogs.docx.md`; `EventDocuments/11-19-complaint-to-dept-of-commerce.pdf` timeline)*

8. **The family was caught in a circular refusal to discuss the report.** Agent Pete advised the family to contact Prieve directly; Prieve refused and told the family to talk to Nic Brau; Nic Brau refused to meet. No one at Farmers would discuss the report or its conclusions with the family. *(Source: `CaseContext/key-facts.md` §2; family's contemporaneous account — exact dates not documented)*

## Phase 5 — Administrative Complaints & Appeals (November 2024 – February 2025)

1. **The family filed a formal complaint with the Minnesota Department of Commerce on 11/19/2024.** Within roughly two weeks of the 11/4 second denial, the family escalated to the state regulator, submitting a written complaint summarizing the claim history, the two denials, and Farmers' refusal to discuss the Prieve report. *(Source: `EventDocuments/11-19-complaint-to-dept-of-commerce.pdf`)*

2. **The family submitted a detailed written appeal on 1/31/2025 with supporting attachments.** The appeal package included (a) the main response letter, (b) a dedicated rebuttal addressing Prieve's "no storms after 2020" weather claim with stormersite data showing hail near Eden Prairie in 2022, 2023, and 2024, and (c) documentation of post-2021 gutter replacement bearing hail damage. *(Sources: `EventDocuments/2025-01-31-ResponseToFarmer.pdf`; `EventDocuments/2025-01-31-ResponseToFarmer-01-31-25-StormsAfter2020.pdf`)*

3. **City of Eden Prairie public permit records independently document hail-driven exterior repairs in the immediate neighborhood after 2020.** The family pulled City building-permit records showing, among others, permit EP187829 at 17490 George Moran Dr (window replacement, issued 11/15/2023, cause: hail) and permit EP190835 at 17708 George Moran Dr (window replacement, issued 5/9/2024, cause: hail). These are official government records from neighboring properties on the same street, independently corroborating hail activity in the exact neighborhood during the time window Prieve claimed was hail-free. *(Source: `EventDocuments/2025-02-10-SecondResponseToFinalReport.pdf`)*

4. **A neighbor canvass found 14 of 19 neighboring homeowners confirmed hail-driven repairs after 2020.** The family went door-to-door in the immediate neighborhood; of 19 homeowners contacted, 14 confirmed their homes had sustained hail damage and undergone hail-related repairs in the same post-2020 period Prieve characterized as storm-free. *(Source: `EventDocuments/2025-02-10-SecondResponseToFinalReport.pdf`)*

5. **Farmers refused to engage with the new evidence the family submitted.** After the 1/31 appeal and the 2/10 second response were submitted, Nic Brau left a voicemail on or about 2/10/2025 declining further review, and Farmers Customer Relations rep Bradlee Monson refused on 2/3/2025 to reopen or reconsider the claim in light of the new documentation. *(Sources: `EventDocuments/Copy of ComunicationLogs.docx.md`; `EventDocuments/2025-02-10-SecondResponseToFinalReport.pdf`)*

6. **Farmers articulated an "engineer's report only" gatekeeping rule and applied it to reject the family's new evidence without reviewing it on the merits.** On 2/3/2025 Farmers field claim supervisor Bradlee Monson told the family by phone that Farmers would not review any evidence or documents from the family unless they were provided by an engineer, adding that "extruded aluminum windows are easily damaged." One week later, on 2/10/2025 at 11:06 AM, Farmers adjuster Nic Brau left a voicemail to the family stating his "field claim supervisor ... did receive the information that you just sent in recently but it's not an engineers report so I believe that's what he wanted to look for from you." The same gatekeeping rule was stated by two different Farmers employees within seven days — first as an affirmative rule by the supervisor and then as the reason for ignoring the family's neighbor canvass, City permit data, and weather records. Nothing in the policy requires evidence to come from an engineer. *(Sources: family's contemporaneous account of the 2/3/2025 phone call with Bradlee Monson — quote not preserved in a Farmers document; `EventDocuments/2025-02-10-TheyDon_tLookAtAnyNewFactsEvidence1.PNG` (Brau voicemail transcription); `CaseContext/key-facts.md` §5)*

7. **The Minnesota Department of Commerce escalated the matter internally but lacked authority to compel Farmers to reverse its denial.** The Commerce complaint moved up to the director level, but the agency's role is regulatory oversight rather than adjudication of individual coverage disputes, so it could not order Farmers to pay or reopen. *(Source: `EventDocuments/Copy of ComunicationLogs.docx.md`; Commerce correspondence referenced in timeline)*

## Phase 7 — Appraisal Process (August–September 2025)

1. **The family invoked the policy's appraisal clause on 8/3/2025 under Minn. Stat. § 65A.01.** The family filed a formal Demand for Appraisal with Farmers naming claim 7007986837-1-1 and loss date 7/13/2024, listing three items in dispute: Windows, Siding (repaint), Siding (R&R). The Sworn Statement on Proof of Loss filed with the demand states a whole loss and damage of $310,000 against the $886,000 Coverage A policy, with a $17,720 deductible. This is the family's affirmative invocation of the contractual dispute-resolution mechanism after Farmers' repeated refusals to reconsider. *(Source: `EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/AppraisalDemand.pdf`)*

2. **The 9/12/2025 Appraisal Award confirmed hail as the cause of loss on the windows.** The Appraisal Award form, signed by umpire Wade Roos and insured's appraiser John Foster, awarded Windows (F1.7 / B1.2) with a Replacement Cost Value of $12,800 and an Actual Cash Value of $6,400. After months of Farmers maintaining that no compensable hail damage existed on any windows beyond the two already paid in 2020, a neutral umpire and the insured's appraiser jointly agreed on the record that hail damage to additional windows is present. *(Sources: `EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/Appraisal_Result_Front.png`; `EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/Appraisal_Result_Back.png`)*

3. **Farmers' own appraiser Greg Tomes did not sign the Appraisal Award.** The award form bears signatures from Foster (insured's appraiser) and Roos (umpire) but not from Tomes. Under Minn. Stat. § 65A.01, an award signed by any two of the three panel members is binding, so Tomes's non-signature did not prevent the award — but it is a factual indicator that Farmers' appointed appraiser would not join in confirming hail damage to the disputed windows. *(Sources: `EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/Appraisal_Result_Front.png`; `EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/AppraisalDemand.pdf` p. 4 (Minn. Stat. § 65A.01 text))*

4. **The Appraisal Award awarded $0 for siding (repaint) and $0 for siding (R&R).** Of the three disputed items, only Windows received an award; both siding items came back at zero. The family's appraisal outcome was partial — a win on causation for windows and a loss on siding. *(Source: `EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/Appraisal_Result_Front.png`)*

5. **The net payment to the family after the Appraisal Award was $0, because the $17,720 deductible exceeds the $6,400 ACV on the only awarded item.** The Additional Information page of the award shows a Payment Summary that nets out to $0.00 after the policy deductible is applied. Farmers' years of resistance and its appraiser's refusal to sign therefore funnel the family into a technically "successful" appraisal that delivers no cash recovery, despite confirmation that hail damage exists. *(Source: `EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/Appraisal_Result_Back.png`)*

6. **The family did not select its own appraiser — Pure Construction chose John Foster.** The Appraiser Retainer Agreement dated 8/3/2025 was issued by Pure Construction (contact Zach Sexe) and retained Foster at $2,200. The family signed the retainer but did not interview or vet Foster, and later learned Foster had previously worked for insurance companies. This is relevant context for how the insured's appraiser ended up with an outcome (only two windows awarded, siding at $0) that walks very close to Farmers' pre-appraisal position. *(Source: `EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/AppraisalDemand.pdf` p. 2 (Appraiser Retainer Agreement))*

7. **Farmers agreed to the appraisal process within the statutory deadline.** There is no evidence in the record that Farmers refused to participate or missed the 20-day appraiser-selection window under § 65A.01. Farmers named Greg Tomes as its appraiser and the process went forward on schedule to the 9/12 inspection and award. *(Sources: `EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/AppraisalDemand.pdf` (Minn. Stat. § 65A.01 text); `EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/Appraisal_Result_Front.png`)*

8. **The Appraisal Award contains no item-by-item itemization, identifying the awarded windows only by the cryptic codes "F1.7" and "B1.2."** Minn. Stat. § 65A.01 Subd. 3 requires the appraisers to "appraise the loss, **stating separately actual value and loss to each item**." The award form does not identify individual windows by location, elevation, sash, or manufacturer stamp — it lists only the undefined shorthand "F1.7" and "B1.2" against a lump-sum $12,800 RCV. The insured cannot determine from the face of the award which windows are covered, and had to email Farmers afterward just to ask what the codes meant. A statutory award that the insured cannot decode on its face arguably fails § 65A.01's "stating separately ... to each item" requirement. *(Sources: `EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/Appraisal_Result_Front.png`; `EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/AppraisalDemand.pdf` p. 4 (Minn. Stat. § 65A.01 text); `EventDocuments/Copy of ComunicationLogs.docx.md` (9/18/2025 email asking Farmers to define F1.7 and B1.2))*

9. **The "insured's" appraiser John Foster was selected by contractor Pure Construction, not by the family, and has a prior insurance-industry background.** Minn. Stat. § 65A.01 Subd. 3 requires each party to select a "competent and **disinterested** appraiser." The family did not interview or vet Foster; contractor Pure Construction picked him and presented the retainer, which the family signed at $2,200. The family later learned Foster previously worked for insurance companies. The insured's appraiser's independence from insurer-side interests is a threshold element of a legitimate § 65A.01 proceeding, and Foster's selection process and background cut against the "disinterested" requirement on the insured side. *(Sources: `EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/AppraisalDemand.pdf` pp. 1–2 (Demand for Appraisal and Appraiser Retainer Agreement); `EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/AppraisalDemand.pdf` p. 4 (Minn. Stat. § 65A.01 text); family's contemporaneous account regarding Foster's insurance-industry history)*

Combined with the facts that (a) Foster was picked by Pure Construction rather than by the family and (b) Foster has a prior insurance-industry background, this $0/$0 siding outcome is a pattern indicator that the insured's appraiser did not meaningfully advocate the insured's scope on siding. *(Source: `EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/Appraisal_Result_Front.png`)*

## 1. Legal Basis

### 1.1 The case

*Quade v. Secura Ins.*, 814 N.W.2d 703 (Minn. 2012), is the controlling Minnesota Supreme Court authority on what an appraisal panel decides under the Standard Fire Policy's appraisal clause. The insureds in *Quade* sustained wind and hail damage and demanded appraisal under their policy's clause; the dispute on appeal was whether the appraisal panel had authority not just to value the loss but to decide what *caused* it — given that Secura had asserted certain damage was pre-existing or attributable to non-covered causes.

The Minnesota Supreme Court held two things that matter for this case:

1. **The phrase "amount of loss," as used in the standard appraisal clause, "unambiguously permits the appraiser to determine the cause of the loss."** The Court reasoned that "loss" in an insurance context inherently incorporates causation: there is no "amount of loss" without first identifying which damages were caused by the insured peril. Causation is therefore not a question reserved for the court; it is part of what the panel decides when it appraises "the loss."
2. **Appraisers must "necessarily determine the cause of the loss as well as the amount necessary to repair the loss."** The opinion ties causation and valuation together as the integrated job of the panel under § 65A.01, Subd. 3.

Coverage questions — whether a particular exclusion applies as a matter of policy interpretation, whether an endorsement modifies the coverage grant — remain with the court. *Quade* draws the line at *factual* causation (what physically caused the damage) versus *legal* coverage (what the policy says about it).

### 1.2 What *Quade* does affirmatively for the insured

When an appraisal panel decides that an insured peril (e.g., hail) caused specific damage and incorporates that finding into a binding two-of-three award under § 65A.01, the insurer is bound by that causation finding. It cannot, in subsequent litigation, relitigate causation under the guise of asserting a non-causation exclusion (wear/tear, marring, corrosion, inherent vice, neglect, faulty construction). Those exclusions are themselves causation defenses dressed in policy language — they say the damage was caused by something *other than* the insured peril. If the panel has already found the damage was caused by the insured peril, the insurer's contrary causation theory has already been adjudicated and lost.

This is the affirmative use of *Quade*: it functions as an issue-preclusion / contractual-finality doctrine that locks in the panel's causation finding and forecloses the insurer from re-arguing the same factual question through a different label in court. The Property Insurance Coverage Law Blog summarizes the rule succinctly: "[w]hen you agree to appraisal, you are agreeing to be bound by the appraisal panel's findings, even if those findings do not go your way," and "in jurisdictions where the 'amount of loss' includes the consideration of whether the damage was caused by a covered cause of loss as opposed to a non-covered cause of loss like wear and tear or pre-existing conditions, the appraisal award will be binding, and if those issues were determined in the appraisal, the insurer will be precluded from litigating those issues."

### 1.3 The statutory backstop: § 65A.01, Subd. 3

The binding force of the panel's findings comes from Minnesota's Standard Fire Policy appraisal clause, embedded verbatim in this policy: "An award in writing, so itemized, of any two when filed with this company shall determine the amount of actual value and loss." *(Minn. Stat. § 65A.01, Subd. 3, reproduced at `EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/AppraisalDemand.pdf` p. 4.)* Because *Quade* reads "amount of loss" to include causation, the same statutory finality language that binds the panel's dollar figure also binds its causation finding. Two-of-three signatures (Foster + Roos) make the 9/12/2025 award final and conclusive on both the amount and the cause.

### 1.4 What *Quade* does NOT do

Three limits matter for E1's correct framing:

- ***Quade* does not increase the dollar award for windows the panel did evaluate.** The award is binding *as the amount* — $12,800 RCV / $6,400 ACV for "Windows (F1.7 / B1.2)." *Quade* locks in causation; it does not let the family argue the panel's amount was wrong without going through the *Mork* / *Herll* / *Rymer* mechanisms covered in `LegalReferences/LegalArguments/A3.md` and `LegalReferences/appraisal_vacatur_caselaw.md`.
- ***Quade* does not bind Farmers as to windows the panel did NOT evaluate.** The panel's causation finding extends only to the items the panel actually appraised. For every other window at 17756 George Moran Dr., the wear/tear, marring, corrosion, inherent vice, neglect, and faulty-construction exclusions cited in the 11/4/2024 denial remain live defenses Farmers can assert in court — and the family bears the burden of proving hail causation independently. *(See `LegalReferences/appraisal_vacatur_caselaw.md` §3 (Honest Re-assessment) on the same point: "Quade does make the hail-as-cause determination final for the items the panel actually evaluated. It does not eliminate coverage defenses for windows the panel never evaluated.")*
- ***Quade* does not displace coverage questions.** Whether J6944 (Limited Matching of Undamaged Property) applies, whether the policy requires "comparable material and quality" replacement when Semco is defunct, and whether other policy exclusions apply as a matter of *interpretation* (not causation) remain for the court. *Cedar Bluff* and *Noonan* control matching; *Quade* is silent on it.

So E1 is a doctrine of preclusion that does specific, narrow work: it kills the six causation-style exclusions Farmers cited in the 11/4/2024 denial, but only for the two windows the panel evaluated.

### 1.5 Procedural posture — how this argument shows up in a complaint

E1 is not pleaded as its own count. It is invoked as a legal premise inside the breach-of-contract count (A1) and the declaratory-judgment count (A2), and in opposition to any Farmers motion or defense that would resurrect the 11/4/2024 exclusions as to the awarded windows. In substance, the pleading uses E1 to say:

> "On September 12, 2025, the appraisal panel issued a binding award under Minn. Stat. § 65A.01 finding hail damage to insured Windows F1.7 and B1.2 with a Replacement Cost Value of $12,800. Under *Quade v. Secura Ins.*, 814 N.W.2d 703 (Minn. 2012), the panel's determination of 'amount of loss' necessarily includes causation, and the panel's causation finding is binding on Defendant. Defendant is therefore precluded from asserting in this action that the F1.7 and B1.2 windows were damaged by wear and tear, marring, corrosion, deterioration, decay, rust, inherent vice, latent defect, neglect, lack of maintenance, or faulty or defective construction — i.e., the Section I.A.7 / I.B.8, I.A.8, I.A.9 / I.B.9, I.A.4 / I.B.6, I.B.4, and I.B.5 exclusions cited in Defendant's November 4, 2024 denial letter."

The same argument is also the substantive answer to any Farmers affirmative defense or summary-judgment motion that tries to revive any of those exclusions for the F1.7 and B1.2 windows.

## 2. Overall Strength Assessment for an Initial Claim

### 2.1 Rating: **80 / 100** as a foundational legal premise; **30 / 100** if scored as a standalone claim

The split rating reflects what E1 actually is.

- **As a doctrine of preclusion to be invoked inside A1, A2, and any opposition to Farmers' affirmative defenses: ~80/100.** *Quade* is bedrock Minnesota Supreme Court law, recently affirmed and routinely applied. The 9/12/2025 award is in the record, binding under § 65A.01, and identifies hail as the cause of loss to the F1.7 and B1.2 windows. The legal logic chain is short and clean: appraisal panel decides amount-of-loss including causation; panel decided hail; insurer is bound. There is no realistic Farmers counter on the doctrine itself.
- **As a standalone claim or argument that wins the case on its own: ~30/100.** E1 only locks in causation for two specific windows valued at $12,800 RCV. That figure is below the $17,720 deductible by $4,920. Even fully credited, E1 by itself produces $0 net recovery. The dollar value of E1 lives entirely in (a) defeating Farmers' six causation-style exclusions for the F1.7 and B1.2 windows, (b) supplying a narrative anchor that hail demonstrably did damage at this property on this date, and (c) strengthening the *Herll*/*Rymer* clarification request (Track 1 of A1) and the bad-faith narrative (B1) by exposing how thin Farmers' original denial theories were.

Weighted blend, reflecting how E1 is realistically used (as a foundational premise for A1 and A2, not as a standalone): **~80/100** for its intended function.

### 2.2 What drives the rating up

- ***Quade* is settled Minnesota Supreme Court law from 2012 and has not been disturbed.** No Minnesota or Eighth Circuit case has narrowed it. *Property Insurance Coverage Law Blog* and *Zelle LLP* both treat *Quade* as the uncontested rule. Farmers cannot argue the doctrine away.
- **The award identifies hail as the cause of loss on its face.** The 9/12/2025 award form lists "Windows (F1.7 / B1.2)" and was issued in response to a Demand for Appraisal that named the cause of loss as hail under claim 7007986837-1-1. The Sworn Statement on Proof of Loss filed on 8/3/2025 also stated hail as the cause. The two-of-three signatures (Foster, Roos) made the award binding under § 65A.01, Subd. 3. *(EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/Appraisal_Result_Front.png; EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/AppraisalDemand.pdf)*
- **The 11/4/2024 denial's six exclusions are all causation-flavored.** Wear and tear, marring, corrosion/deterioration/decay/rust, inherent vice/latent defect, neglect/lack of maintenance, and faulty/defective construction are not coverage-grant exclusions; they are alternative-causation theories. *Quade* foreclosure of "what caused the loss" simultaneously forecloses all six as to the windows the panel evaluated. *(EventDocuments/FarmersDenyLetter-11-4.pdf p. 3)*
- **Farmers' own pre-appraisal expert (Prieve) staked a different causation theory that the panel rejected.** Prieve's report attributed the dents to "wear/tear," to "blunt object" impact, to construction-era damage, and to "marring," and asserted that "most recent hail in Eden Prairie reported in 2020" — i.e., no recent hail. The panel's binding hail-causation finding is a direct on-the-record rejection of Prieve's framework for the F1.7 and B1.2 windows. *(EventDocuments/FarmersDenyLetter-11-4.pdf pp. 4–10)*
- **Farmers also affirmatively walked into appraisal.** Farmers participated in § 65A.01 appraisal, named Greg Tomes as its appraiser within the 20-day window, and accepted the umpire selection and process. Farmers therefore agreed, in advance, that the appraisal panel would decide the amount of loss — and under *Quade* "amount of loss" includes causation. Farmers cannot now argue it never agreed to give the panel that authority. *(EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/AppraisalDemand.pdf; EventDocuments/Appraisal Documents/Appraisal_Result_Front.png)*

### 2.3 What drives the rating down

- **The dollar value of pure E1 preclusion is small.** The award is $12,800 RCV / $6,400 ACV — below the $17,720 deductible. E1 standing alone delivers $0 net. The argument is foundational, not financially decisive.
- **Preclusion is narrow to the F1.7 and B1.2 windows.** For every other window the family says was hail-damaged, Farmers' six exclusions remain live defenses. The family cannot use *Quade* as a global shortcut; it has to either (a) get the panel's scope expanded by remand under *Herll*/*Rymer* (in which case the *Quade* preclusion expands with it), or (b) prove hail causation for the unevaluated windows independently in court.
- **The award does not itemize what the panel actually evaluated and what it excluded.** The codes "F1.7" and "B1.2" are not defined on the award form. Farmers' own descriptions of which windows the codes refer to have been mutually inconsistent (9/18/2025 "front right, and rear elevation in the dining room"; 9/19/2025 acknowledged that was wrong; 10/14/2025 repeated the same description). A court enforcing *Quade* preclusion needs to know what specifically the panel found — and the award is sparse. Some clarification under *Herll*/*Rymer* may be needed before E1 has its full preclusive force. *(EventDocuments/Copy of ComunicationLogs.docx.md (9/18, 9/19, 10/14/2025 entries); `LegalReferences/LegalArguments/A3.md` §3.1)*
- **E1 is structurally incompatible with full *Mork* vacatur.** If the family succeeds on a *Mork* attack that vacates the entire 9/12/2025 award (Tier 3 of A3), the causation finding goes with it and *Quade* preclusion disappears. The family then has to prove hail causation from scratch for every window, and Farmers can revive its six exclusions for everything. E1 therefore creates a strategic constraint on the A3 architecture: lead with *Herll*/*Rymer* clarification (which preserves causation), then statutory-noncompliance modification (which can preserve causation), and reserve full vacatur for facts discovery actually delivers. The same point is made in `LegalReferences/LegalArguments/A3.md` §4.
- ***Quade* does not displace J6944.** Matching is a coverage question (A2/A1 Track 2), not a causation question. *Quade* is silent on it. The matching battle still has to be fought on the *Cedar Bluff* / *Noonan* axis with J6944's text. E1 is not a substitute.
- **No on-record explanation of the panel's causation reasoning exists.** The umpire produced no report, estimate, or methodology. *(EventDocuments/Copy of ComunicationLogs.docx.md (9/24/2025 entry).)* That gap does not defeat the binding force of the award (the form itself is enough under § 65A.01), but it limits how much detail E1's preclusive scope can be argued to carry.

### 2.4 What "80/100" means practically

E1 is the legal premise that converts an appraisal award the family did not financially win into a defensive shield with concrete preclusive value. The doctrine itself is rock-solid; the limits come from the narrowness of what was actually decided and the dollar inadequacy of the awarded amount. Treat E1 as a tool inside A1 and A2 — not as a freestanding count, and not as the lever that breaks the deductible. Its job is to (a) eliminate Farmers' six causation exclusions for two specific windows, (b) lock in a narrative finding that hail demonstrably caused damage at the property, and (c) feed the *Herll*/*Rymer* request and the bad-faith narrative.

## 4. Does This Argument Depend on Vacating, Modifying, or Correcting the Appraisal?

### 4.1 Short answer

**The opposite. E1 affirmatively depends on the 9/12/2025 appraisal award being UPHELD.** The argument is not standalone in the sense of being independent of the appraisal — it is the legal consequence that follows from the award standing as binding under § 65A.01. If the family vacates the award entirely, *Quade* preclusion goes with it and Farmers' six 11/4/2024 exclusions all return as live defenses for every window.

This makes E1 different in posture from A1, A2, and A3:

- **A1 (breach of contract)** can be filed regardless of whether the award stands or falls — it just changes which Track is dominant.
- **A2 (declaratory judgment)** can be filed regardless.
- **A3 (vacate / modify / correct)** affirmatively asks the court to disturb the award, in tiered relief.
- **E1** sits in tension with the most aggressive A3 sub-claim (Tier 3 — full vacatur under *Mork*) and is fully compatible with the others (Tier 1 *Herll*/*Rymer* remand for clarification; Tier 2 modification/correction under § 65A.01, Subd. 3).

### 4.2 Compatibility with the A3 architecture

| A3 sub-claim | What it does to the award | Effect on E1 |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 — *Herll*/*Rymer* remand for clarification | Award stays in place; panel clarifies scope of what it evaluated and why | **Compatible — and synergistic.** Clarification expands or pins down which windows the panel found hail-damaged; *Quade* preclusion attaches to whatever the clarified scope is. Each additional window the panel clarifies it included expands E1's preclusive bite. |
| Tier 2 — Modification / correction to comply with § 65A.01 Subd. 3's itemization requirement | Award stays in substance; the form is corrected to itemize | **Compatible.** Itemization makes the *Quade* causation finding clearer for each item and easier to enforce. |
| Tier 3 — Full vacatur under *Mork* | Award is set aside | **Incompatible.** Vacatur erases the panel's causation finding, so *Quade* preclusion disappears. Farmers' six exclusions return as live defenses for every window. |

The strategic implication is the same as `A3.md` §4 reaches by the other route: lead the appraisal attack with Tiers 1 and 2 (clarification and itemization), and reserve Tier 3 (vacatur) for the discovery facts that would make it provable. E1 reinforces that prioritization — the family loses *Quade* preclusion the moment full vacatur succeeds. Tier 3 should never be the lead theory, and if it ever does become viable, the family will have to weigh whether the dollar gain from disturbing the $12,800 figure outweighs the loss of *Quade* preclusion on causation.

### 4.3 Compatibility with the rest of the case

- **A1 (breach of contract) — fully compatible.** E1 is one of the legal premises that drives A1's "Track 1" (unevaluated windows) and "Track 2" (matching) framing. The award stands, *Quade* binds Farmers on causation for the F1.7/B1.2 windows, and A1 then seeks coverage for the unevaluated windows under *Quade*-coverage doctrine, plus matching relief.
- **A2 (declaratory judgment) — fully compatible.** Asking the court to declare the scope of coverage uses E1 as the foundation: the panel established hail causation for the F1.7/B1.2 windows; the court is asked to declare that the same exclusions cannot defeat coverage as to those windows, that Farmers' "engineer's report only" gatekeeping has no policy basis, and that any matching obligation under *Cedar Bluff* applies despite J6944's limits.
- **B1 (bad faith under § 604.18) — synergistic in narrative, complicated procedurally.** *Quade* preclusion sharpens the bad-faith narrative: Farmers issued two denials grounded in causation theories its own appraisal panel rejected. That helps Prong 1 (objective unreasonableness) and Prong 2 (knowledge / reckless disregard). But the appraisal-bar in § 604.18 Subd. 4(b) is a separate question — see `LegalReferences/LegalArguments/B1.md`. E1 itself does not affect that procedural bar.
- **B4 (estoppel / waiver of denial rationales) — compatible.** *Quade* preclusion is a closely related doctrine: where E1 says Farmers cannot relitigate the panel's causation finding, B4 says Farmers' own shifting denials estop it from reasserting positions inconsistent with prior denials. The two arguments work in parallel.

### 4.4 Where E1's dependency actually bites

The dependency is one-directional. E1 is not an attack on the award; it is an enforcement of the award's preclusive force. The two practical consequences are:

1. **The family must not pursue full vacatur as the lead theory.** A successful Tier 3 *Mork* attack would destroy E1. The strategic ranking in `A3.md` §4 already reflects this — E1 reinforces it.
2. **The family must affirmatively defend the validity of the award when relevant.** If Farmers (in some other procedural posture) tries to argue the award is non-binding or void, the family loses *Quade* preclusion. This is unlikely on the current record — Farmers has affirmatively relied on the binding character of the award in its 10/21/2025 communication — but it is a posture the family should be ready to maintain.

### 4.5 Bottom line on the key question

- **E1 does not require vacating, modifying, or correcting the appraisal — it requires the appraisal to be UPHELD.**
- E1 is therefore standalone as to the question of whether the appraisal needs to be disturbed: it does not need to be disturbed for E1 to operate.
- E1 is *dependent* in the opposite direction: it lives or dies with the award's continued binding force. Full vacatur destroys it; *Herll*/*Rymer* clarification or § 65A.01 itemization correction strengthen it.
- E1 is best paired with A1, A2, and the Tier-1/Tier-2 sub-claims of A3. It is in tension with Tier-3 *Mork* vacatur and with any strategy that invites the court to set the award aside in full.
